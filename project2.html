Brand Creation [CHEN OL' DUCK / 陳老鴨研究所]

Service : Branding | Role : Graphic Designer

This project was completed while I worked part-time in a Branding agency in Richmond: PLAYHEADS. It was a branding project for a duck-meat snack bar in Richmond, located in front of the Aberdeen center. The inspiration behind this creation was to create something unique for this restaurant. Since there are many competitors in the market. After doing some market research, we found that the majority of people who are purchasing duck products are young generations and people around 16-35. So our team decided to go with something more appealing to young generations. We started out our design brainstorm on flat drawing logos, with some color contrast.

We had several designers working together to complete this project, as one of the graphic designers, we first split into individuals and came up without our own mood board. We shared our progress and eliminate the selections into two and presented them to the client. After the client's approval, we took the logo that the client was most satisfied with and created a high fidelity version with some product mockups.

What was challenging as a graphic designer was to brainstorm the ideas for this brand? We wanted something fresh and young. However, we did not want to avoid the minority of customers. We also wanted something that would look good, fun, too easy to memorize, so whenever customers purchase products, they will then remember this brand. I enjoy finding inspirations online also look through branding creation books for incentives, which always help you expand to the more broad ideas.

About CHEN OL' DUCK / 關於陳老鴨研究所

CHEN OL’ DUCK is a Vancouver based food & beverage brand with nearly a decade of experience in personal chef services. Now facing a high demand, they are opening up their first restaurant in Richmond. The creation behind CHEN OL’ DUCK contains high contrast design and symbolic grahics. With the rebarnding of this restaurant it targets more of the young generation.
